Complete Plant Care Guide

Research-backed advice to help every plant in your home thrive — from Tulsi to succulents, through every Indian season.

Basics By Plant Seasonal Care Pest Control Propagation Common Issues
water_drop

Watering

Most indoor plants prefer their soil to dry out slightly between waterings. Stick your finger 2–3 inches (5 cm) into the soil — if it feels dry, it's time to water.

lightbulb Pro Tips

  • Over-watering is the #1 killer of houseplants
  • Always use pots with drainage holes
  • Water early morning for best absorption
  • Reduce watering frequency in monsoon & winter
Indoor Succulents Tropical
light_mode

Light

Bright, indirect light works best for most foliage plants. South or east-facing windows are ideal in India. Avoid direct afternoon sun which can scorch leaves — especially during Indian summers.

lightbulb Indian Light Guide

  • North window: Low light — ZZ, Snake Plant
  • East window: Gentle morning sun — Pothos, Ferns
  • West window: Harsh afternoon — diffuse with curtains
  • South window: Bright all day — succulents, Tulsi
Low Light Bright Indirect Direct Sun
thermostat

Temperature

Most houseplants thrive between 18–30°C, which suits most Indian homes perfectly. Keep plants away from AC vents, drafty windows, and direct heater exposure.

lightbulb Key Points

  • Sudden temperature drops below 10°C can harm Tulsi
  • Move plants away from AC vents in summer
  • Group plants together in winter for warmth
18–30°C Avoid Drafts
opacity

Humidity

Many indoor plants, especially tropical varieties, love humidity. During dry months or in AC rooms, increase moisture by misting leaves, using a pebble tray, or grouping plants together.

lightbulb For Indian Homes

  • Monsoon = natural humidity boost (no misting needed)
  • AC rooms dry the air — mist frequently in summer
  • Bathrooms & kitchens are naturally more humid
Tropical Plants Misting
pot

Soil & Potting

Use well-draining potting mix tailored to your plant type. Cacti and succulents need sandy, fast-draining soil, while ferns and tropicals prefer moisture-retentive mixes with organic matter.

lightbulb DIY Mix Guide

  • Succulents: 2 parts soil + 1 part sand + 1 part perlite
  • Indoor foliage: 3 parts soil + 1 part coco peat + 1 part compost
  • Repot every 12–18 months to refresh nutrients
  • Always use pots with drainage holes
Well-draining Repotting
nutrition

Fertilizing

Feed plants with a balanced liquid fertilizer every 2–4 weeks during the growing season (spring & summer). Reduce to once every 6–8 weeks in fall and winter. Always follow package instructions — more is not better.

lightbulb Organic Options

  • Banana peel water: Soak peels 48 hrs, dilute 1:5 — rich in potassium
  • Vermicompost: Top-dress every 4–6 weeks in growing season
  • Well-decomposed cow dung: Classic Indian organic fertilizer
  • Avoid fertilizing during monsoon & winter dormancy

Seasonal Care Calendar

Adjust your plant care routine as the seasons change.

wb_sunny Summer (Mar–Jun)
  • Water: Increase frequency; check soil daily
  • Light: Provide shade from harsh afternoon sun (12–3 PM)
  • Humidity: Mist frequently, especially in AC rooms
  • Fertilize: Active growing season — feed every 2 weeks
  • Position: Move plants a few feet away from hot windows
  • Extra: Group plants to create humidity microclimate
thunderstorm Monsoon (Jul–Oct)
  • Water: Reduce drastically — soil stays wet longer
  • Drainage: Ensure pots don't sit in water; elevate on bricks
  • Airflow: Prune overcrowded branches to prevent fungus
  • Fertilize: Stop fertilizing until post-monsoon
  • Fungus: Remove yellow/fallen leaves from soil surface
  • Pests: Watch for fungus gnats and mealybugs
ac_unit Winter (Nov–Feb)
  • Water: Reduce significantly — once a week or less
  • Light: Move plants closer to windows for maximum light
  • Temp: Bring sensitive plants indoors; protect from <10°C
  • Grouping: Place pots together to create warmth microclimate
  • Fertilize: Stop fertilizing — plants are dormant
  • Extra: Reduce misting; avoid cold drafts from windows

Natural Pest Control

Fight common plant pests without harsh chemicals. These DIY remedies are safe, effective, and use ingredients available in every Indian kitchen.

eco

🌿 Neem Oil Spray

Best for: Aphids, mealybugs, spider mites, whiteflies, fungus

Recipe (1 litre):

  • 1 litre warm water
  • 2 tsp (5 ml) pure neem oil
  • 2–3 drops mild liquid soap (emulsifier)

Shake well before use. Spray on both sides of leaves every 5–7 days. Use within 8 hours of mixing. Apply early morning or late evening.

soap

🧼 Soap & Water Spray

Best for: Aphids, mealybugs, scale insects (soft-bodied pests)

Recipe (1 litre):

  • 1 litre water
  • 1 tbsp Castile soap or mild dish soap

Suffocates soft-bodied insects on contact. Avoid excessive use as it can remove the plant's natural wax coating. Rinse after 2 hours.

garlic

🧄 Garlic Repellent Spray

Best for: Deterring beetles, aphids, and fungal infections

Recipe (1 litre):

  • 2 minced garlic cloves
  • 1 litre water
  • Strain before use

Let steep for 24 hours for maximum potency. Spray weekly as a preventative measure. Works as a natural fungicide too!

⚠️ Important

Always test any spray on a small leaf first. Apply in early morning or late evening to prevent leaf burn. Never spray plants in direct sunlight.

Propagation Pro Tips

1

Always cut below a node

Nodes contain the growing cells roots need. Cut at a 45° angle for better water absorption.

2

Let cuttings callous

For succulents and snake plants, let the cut end dry for 2 days before planting to prevent rot.

3

Change water weekly

If rooting in water, change the water every week to prevent bacterial growth and rotting.

4

Wait before fertilizing

New cuttings need no fertilizer for the first 4 weeks. Let roots establish first.

Common Issues & Solutions

Don't worry if your plant looks unhappy — most problems are easy to fix with the right diagnosis.

Most common cause: Over-watering and poor drainage.

Solution: Let the soil dry out before watering again. Check that your pot has drainage holes. If roots are mushy and black, you may need to repot with fresh soil after trimming rotten roots.

Most common cause: Dry air (low humidity) or inconsistent watering.

Solution: Increase humidity by misting, using a pebble tray, or grouping plants. In AC rooms, place a small water bowl nearby. Trim brown tips with clean scissors at an angle.

Two possible causes: Under-watering or over-watering. Check soil moisture to determine which.

If dry soil: Water thoroughly and the plant should perk up within hours. If wet soil: Over-watering — stop watering and let soil dry out. Your plant may have root rot.

Signs: Sticky residue, tiny webs, white cottony masses, or tiny bugs on leaf undersides.

Solution:

  1. Isolate affected plant immediately
  2. Wipe leaves with neem oil solution (recipe above)
  3. Remove heavily infested leaves
  4. Repeat treatment every 5–7 days for 3 weeks

Cause: Not enough light — the plant is "reaching" for the sun.

Solution: Move to a brighter location (east-facing window ideal). Prune back elongated stems to encourage bushier growth. Rotate the pot weekly for even growth.

Signs: Plant wilting despite wet soil, mushy dark roots, foul smell.

Treatment:

  1. Remove plant from pot and gently wash roots
  2. Trim all mushy, black, smelly roots with sterilized scissors
  3. Repot in fresh, well-draining soil
  4. Do NOT fertilize for 4 weeks
  5. Water sparingly until you see new growth

Signs: Brown, crispy patches on leaves facing the window, especially in summer.

Solution: Move plant away from direct afternoon sun. Use sheer curtains to diffuse harsh light. Remove severely damaged leaves. Most plants recover with proper light placement.
